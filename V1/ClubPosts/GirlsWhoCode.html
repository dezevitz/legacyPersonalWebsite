Founding Girls Who Code at UVA

January 2020 - Present

What is Girls Who Code at UVA?

According to USNEWS.com, in the 1984 only 37% of computer science majors were female. This number steadly decreased, and now, only 18% of the computer science industry is female.

Most girls lose interest in computer science around the middle school years.

Girls Who Code is a non-profit organization founded by Reshma Saujani dedicated to instilling a passion for coding in female computer scientist at a young age. By providing resources to young women and by normalizing computer science in their education, we can close the gender gap in computer science.

Girls Who Code at UVA contibutes to the cause by assisting local schools and girl scout troups as well as providing its members with unique workshops and opportunities.

My involment

When I was in my Sophomore year of college, my roommate and I founded a chapter of Girls Who Code at our college, the University of Virginia. I took the role of vice president and spent hours everyday making the club what it is today. Within two weeks, we became one of largest GWC loops nationally.

My duties:

  • Leading Executive Member and General Member Meetings
  • Reaching out to external companies and organizations
  • Monitor all club events
  • Create a three year plan that insures the sustainability of the club for years to come
  • Fulfil the presidential duties when the President is not avaliable (mainly maintaining communication and transparency between other GWC chapters and club sponsors)
